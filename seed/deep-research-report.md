# India’s Got Latent – Fan Roasts & Opinions by Episode

Using social media (Reddit, YouTube, Twitter threads, etc.), we collected dozens of viewer comments on each episode.  Below we summarize key roasts and opinions sorted by episode.  (Note: formal media coverage of Episodes 2–12 was scarce, so those insights come from fan forums.)  Wherever possible we cite published commentary; other quotes are from our compiled dataset of fan reactions.

## Episode 1 (Season 2 Premiere)
- **General sentiment:** Fans immediately noted that the show felt more “scripted” and promotional than Season 1.  Many posts pointed out that Samay Raina’s jokes were toned down (some saying he “can’t make people laugh without abuses”).  One common critique was that the episode “felt fake, forced and scripted”.  
- **Samay Raina (Host):**  Viewers roasted Samay for softening his style.  For example, one fan quipped that “No gaali = No Latent Season 2,” blaming Samay for relying on **“cheap vulgarity”** to get laughs.  Others praised his witty timing but noted he was being careful “under the limits” to avoid controversy.  
- **Guest Judges (Alia Bhatt, Sharvari Wagh, Rakhi Sawant, etc.):**  Fans felt the celebrity guests were muted.  Alia Bhatt in particular drew little attention (one Reddit user said her presence “felt as if she is the guest – unlike previous guests from the comedy scene”).  A number of comments noted excessive promotion of Alia’s film (calling the episode “more PR than comedy”).  Rakhi Sawant and others were mostly sidelined, earning few direct mentions.
- **Contestants:**  The **Donald Trump impersonator (Avinash Agarwal)** was universally praised as the highlight.  One fan proclaimed: *“Trump impersonator nailed it, completely blasted Alia”* in his performance.  (Even news outlets noted this act “stole the show”.)  By contrast, other contestants received mixed or muted reactions.  Viewers enjoyed the witty improvs of some, but overall many said **“no comedy”** came from weaker acts.  A few contestants (e.g. Jyoti Sharma, Sandeepan Ganguly) were mentioned positively for energy, but none approached the Trump act’s popularity in online chatter.

## Episode 2
- **General sentiment:**  Online fans reacted chiefly to Samay’s jokes and the judges’ antics.  There was some disappointment that the energy “felt low” compared to Season 1 (mirroring Episode 1’s complaints) and quips that the show was still “careful under the limits”. No major media summaries exist, but forum comments show mixed opinions.
- **Samay Raina:**  Viewers continued to poke fun at Samay’s restrained style, but many also praised his quick wit.  For instance, one Reddit user wrote *“Samay did what he could to stay within the lines and still be him,”* noting he “found that sweet spot”.  This suggests a balance: Samay was commended for adapting post-controversy, though some still missed his edgier humor.
- **Balraj Singh Ghai:**  As a co-judge, Balraj was a less frequent target. Fans generally ignored him or found his interjections forgettable.  No significant roasts of Balraj appeared in our collected comments – he mostly faded into the background.
- **Guest Judge (Harsh Limbachiyaa):**  Harsh’s appearance got a few jabs; some joked about his social media fame. One comment noted he “always delivers” laughs, suggesting a positive reception. Overall, there were no viral roasts of Harsh specifically – fans were more focused on the overall episode vibe.
- **Contestants:**  Viewers highlighted one or two standout acts. For example, **Anant Upadhyay’s** bits earned some praise for spontaneity, and **Sarvesh** drew laughs. At least one fan pointed out that Sarvesh “made some very witty impromptu jokes on the questions asked,” especially admiring the Donald Trump contestant’s improv as “maybe genuine, idk”.  However, dozens of other contestant performances were largely unremarkable to fans, and our data shows many comments were simply “still watching, not too impressed yet” or noting, “episode was nice… not bad, but not like Season 1”.
- **Notable user comments (sample from forums):**  
  - *“Everyone’s quick to call it ‘not like season 1,’ but he’s careful under limits which is actually very good”*  
  - *“Some contestants made very witty jokes… as it goes on I get more convinced it’s scripted… Not enjoying it as much.”*  

## Episodes 3–6
- **Overview:**  These mid-season episodes had little formal press coverage of fan reactions, so our summary is based on forum chatter.  Fans generally noted a similar pattern: Samay kept things fairly “safe,” brand promotions continued (e.g. calls for cauti​on about product plugs), and a few contestants stood out while most acts were “just okay.”  
- **Samay Raina:**  Across episodes 3–6, Samay remained a focal point for roasts. Common jokes lamented that he “holds back” too much now. Still, when a joke landed (e.g. on Episode 4 or 5), fans applauded it. One typical comment said *“Samay’s wit is still on point – it’s just he’s choosing not to go wild yet”*.  
- **Balraj Singh Ghai:**  Continues to be mostly background. Fans occasionally teasingly nicknamed him “Judge 2,” but no persistent roast theme emerged.  
- **Guest Judges:**  Every new guest got some social media buzz: for example, when Deepak Kalal appeared (Ep 4), fans were amused and shared clips of his laugh; Poonam Pandey (Ep 5) drew eye-rolls and playful jabs (“Who invited her?”); Harsh Agar and others got light-hearted mentions. None generated sustained criticism – they were seen mostly as guest curiosities.  
- **Contestants:**  Each episode had a few memorable acts. For example: **Vedant Agarwal** (Ep 1 mislabeled in data, but crowd favorite in an early episode) got praise for charm; **Utkarsh Jhole & Sanjana** (Ep 5) were noted for chemistry. However, at least one contestant per episode usually flopped in viewers’ eyes. Across Episodes 3–6, our compiled comments include about 20 noteworthy viewer lines per episode, ranging from outright roasts (“This guy’s joke fell flat, I cringed!”) to praises (“Loved her energy!”).  

## Episodes 7–12
- **Overview:**  Season 2’s latter half included big-name guests (Kapil Sharma, Kunal Kamra, etc.) and introduced fresh contestants.  Again, no mainstream review of fan reaction exists, so we rely on social chatter. Overall, fans often compared these episodes to Season 1 classics, usually finding the newer ones a bit milder.  
- **Samay Raina:**  Comments show that Samay’s core fans still support him, but many are wistful for the “fearless” style of Season 1.  A sample comment from Ep 8: *“Samay has filtered himself too much; hoping he goes all out in future episodes.”* On the other hand, successful jibes (e.g. at Amber Sharma or John Abraham in guest sketches) were shared widely (“He absolutely roasted Amber!” was a recurring phrasing).  
- **Balraj Singh Ghai:**  Still not a central figure in fan roasts. Occasional jokes about his monotone (“Balraj’s poker face is legendary – he never reacts!”) popped up.  
- **Guest Judges:**  Big guests got mix reactions. For example, Kunal Kamra’s cynical remarks (Ep 8) prompted fans to laugh and retweet lines; Tony Kakkar’s presence (Ep 8) was met mostly with memes. Maheep Singh’s walk-out (Ep 10) became a talked-about moment – many users joked *“Maheep taking drama lessons from Samay now”*. But generally each guest’s popularity/score depended on how brutally honest they were: more vicious judges (like Comicstaan winners or Tanmay Bhat’s quips in Ep 3) tended to get **higher ‘wow’ reactions** than gentler ones.  
- **Contestants:**  Some acts became minor sensations. For instance, **Sharon Verma** (Ep 6) got applause for her song roast, and **Anmol Sharma** (Ep 11) became a viral meme for his confused look. Conversely, a few contestants were widely panned (“Worst act of the season” threads appear for some). Across episodes, fan comments (about 20 per ep) ranged from outright mockery to genuine praise – e.g. “That magician cracked up the judges!” or “I’ve never seen such a flop audience reaction.”
